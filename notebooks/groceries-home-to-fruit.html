Our growers

People behind the produce

We source products from around the world to deliver to your door, click the images below to find out more about some of the people behind our produce

Cherries

Time honoured traditions

Cherries

The Wilkin family has been farming fruits at Tiptree since 1757, being recognised for working sympathetically with the environment. Any fruit leftover is utilised to make quality preserves, which first received Royal Warrant in 1911. Tiptree remains purveyors to HRH Queen Elizabeth II today.

Shop cherries

Apples

Hand picked for you

Organic apples

Organic Farm Foods in Gloucestershire is managed by third generation farmers; father and son, John. They have been growing organic apples since 2004. The apples are picked by hand and packed just hours before being sent to Sainsbury’s.

Shop apples

Sunflowers

Sustainable sunflowers

Sunflowers

Snaith Flowers, based in Lincolnshire have been growing sunflowers for 20 years with over 7 million stems of sunflowers being grown annually. They are SEDEX registered which means that their products are proactively sourced ethically and sustainably.

Shop sunflowers

Potatoes

Potatoes to perfection

TTD White Potatoes

The McLaren family has been growing potatoes since 1938. Douglas is the fourth generation of the family dedicated to growing our TTD potatoes and explained Scotland is the ideal place to grow them due to the soil type, climate, and plenty of water.

Shop potatoes

Celery

Cracking celery crops

Ivaylo Ksotadinov

One of G’s growers, Ivaylo, has been a pivotal part of G’s celery business for the last 15 years, continually developing how it’s produced. From growing longer length celery, maximising available land, to moving to water efficiency solutions, reducing water usage by approximately 30%.

Shop celery

Toiletries & health

Toiletries & health

Incorporating Fruit & Veg into your diet isn’t as hard as you think. There are a variety of ways you can add some colour onto your plate. Need help in getting some vegetables into your children? Eat The Rainbow is an initiative to help people add colour to their meals. Eating fruits and vegetables of different colours can be a great way to introduce your children into healthy eating.

Smoothies are not only extremely tasty, but they’re a great source of vitamins and nutrients, as well as counting towards your five a day. Having them as a refreshment during a workout session, or to have them to boost your energy levels whilst sitting at your desk, they are a great way to integrate fruit and veg into your daily routine.

Wanting to be environmentally friendly? Organic fruit and vegetables are more sustainably grown and stored. So if your impact on the environment is on your conscious, Organic Fruit & Vegetables may be the option for you. Whether you’re having it for dinner on a warm summer’s day or as a light lunch in the office, a salad is perfect for all you healthy eaters
